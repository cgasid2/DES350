Death Valley
CA, NV

Death Valley is the hottest, driest and lowest national park.

It is a land of extremes, yet each has its own contrast.

From peaks frosted with winter snow to rare rainstorms bringing fields of wildflowers.
From tiny fish to places of refuge for wildlife, Death Valley is home to a great diversity of wildlife.

Death Valley also has many ghost towns, one of them being the Rhyolite, known as the Queen City.

Activities
Scene Driving, Hiking
Want to visit Tatooine? The famous movie series, Star Wars, has filmed in the park for Episode IV – A New Hope and Star Wars Episode VI – Return of the Jedi.
Biking and Mountain Biking
Death Valley has more than 785 miles of roads including hundreds of miles suitable for mountain biking.
Tour Scotty's Castle
Scotty's Castle is an elaborate, Spanish-style mansion built in the 1920s and '30s. Take a ranger-guided tour of the castle interior or the system of underground tunnels.
Birdwatching
Death Valley is one of the most impressive ornithological biomes in the National Park System. Migratory birds are channeled into these valleys and stop at its desert oases and mountains, resulting in Death Valley’s high number of bird species.
Backpacking
There are few established trails in the park, but hikers can follow canyon bottoms, open desert washes, alluvial fans and abandoned dirt roads to get around, as well as the opportunity to experience dark night skies, sweeping vistas and awesome geology.
